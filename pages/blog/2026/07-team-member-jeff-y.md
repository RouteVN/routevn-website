## Team Member Introduction: Jeff Y

Jeff Y is a second-year Computer Science student at UNSW. He has experience building JavaScript backend systems and is interested in visual novels and VN tooling.
He joined RouteVN as an intern from December 2025 to March 2026.

### About Jeff Y

Jeff's programming journey started early, with single-chip robot programming in primary school, Scratch in junior high, and Python in senior high.

He is currently focused on growing both frontend and backend engineering skills, and wants to strengthen his ability to collaborate in a small, fast-moving team.

### Experience with Visual Novels

Jeff has read visual novels in both Chinese and English across multiple genres, including science fiction, horror, comedy, and romance.

Some of the visual novels he has played:

- Doki Doki Literature Club!
- Steins;Gate
- Chaos;Child
- Danganronpa
- Phoenix Wright: Ace Attorney Trilogy

### Contributions at RouteVN

Jeff has contributed across RouteVN's core projects:

- **Route Graphics (`route-graphics`)**: Particle system.
- **Route Engine (`route-engine`)**: Many critical features, including menu page, history mode, NVL mode, save/load functionality, and variables.
- **RouteVN Creator Client (`routevn-creator-client`)**: Next-line config, additional layout editor features, and variables page improvements.

### Product Thinking

Jeff is especially interested in developer tools for visual novel players on Linux. One direction he cares about is lightweight tools for data injection and extraction from applications, as an alternative to Windows-only OCR or hook-based solutions.

If he could improve one thing in visual novels, he would prioritize quick route selection through flowcharts. In his view, this helps players quickly revisit past progress after long breaks and reduces repeated scene-skipping when exploring new routes.

He also highlights MTool as a product he admires for its practical all-in-one design for RPG Maker games, useful runtime features, and sustainable monetization.

### Proudest Work

Jeff is most proud of leading a JavaScript backend group project, where he coordinated implementation, reviewed teammates' code, found bugs, and helped the team deliver a strong final result.

Another project he is proud of is a panoramic school map he built in junior high, which was recognized as an outstanding submission.
